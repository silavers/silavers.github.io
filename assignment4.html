Printing

This is where the bulk of the pivots occured.

First, I did a print of the candle base I was considering. This was mostly meant to be a test print to see how it turned out. And it turned out real bad... I left in on overnight and returned to it "printing" with nothing coming out of the nozzle... Turns out the culprit was my filament getting tangled. [[PIC]]

I replaced the filament and did another bed level print after I had cleaned off the misprint. The filament wasn't melting or coming out of the nozzle at all, so I decided to diagnose the clog.

After a few youtube videos, I was able to declog most of it, but probably dis-assembled my printer too much. [[PIC]]

I put the fan, nozzle, and thermistor back together (what I later found was the wrong way).

I waited what seemed like a lot longer than usual for it to start printing.

It started to look like it was going to print and then a high pitched alarm went off and a "Thermal Runway" error message showed on the scren. I quickly turned it off and googled. Thermal Runway error occurs when the printer can't ready the true temperature of the nozzle i.e. it thinks its still room temp, but actually its heating and is like 200 degrees already. This means it could keep heating it until basically it catches fire because it never knows when it has reached the maximum it needs to start printing.

I learned that this is a huge fire hazard and I could have severed the delicate thermistor wires in my printer.

The good news is I also learned that not all 3D printers have the software automatically installed to detect a Thermal Runway, so I was lucky to have caught it!

A few videos that helped me to diagnose it & fix it: Video 1 & Video 2

Hours later, I had the thermistor back in the right spot (gently recoiled around the screw) and a big jug of water next to me in case my printer caught on fire.

A few small test prints showed that the nozzle was heating correctly again and the thermistor was recognizing the temperature correctly. So I went in for my lamp print.

Just when I thought I was in the clear

I set my print up and ... ran out of clear filament about 60% through the print. [[PIC]]

Ok, so back to the drawing board again, I'm lucky I have a big spool of white filament..

Another pivot..

I decided to take this opportunity to simplify my deisgn to focus on the candle, since that would be the light source anyway, and I realized that without the clear filament, I now had a fatal design flaw-- I needed to figure out how to make sure this lamp really lit up the room (pun intended again...)

So I decided to ensure I was printing a hollow candle so the bulb could still light it up. This required some MeshBooleanDifference magic (thanks for your help with that JunChao!)
