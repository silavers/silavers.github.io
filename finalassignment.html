Final Project: Concrete & Metal Jewelry Collection


Concept

For my final project, I made a mini jewelry collection. Inspired by the mix of concrete and metal jewelry trend, and seeing that proto pasta sold metal filament, I thought it would be interesting to see if I could mix the 3d printing with casting.

The concrete jewlery inspiration below all are either painted or include metals. My goal was to include 3D printed metals.

Sources: Necklaces, Ring, Ring Holder

After a few concept sketches, I decided to edit down my pieces to:
1 rectangular pendant with 3d printed piece in it
2 ring holders (1 with 3d printed piece in it)
3 rings (2 with 3d print piece in it)

Process

For every piece, I followed these steps:
- I test printed the pieces first until I got the perfect dimensions
- Made adjustments
- Then test printed the mold
- Made adjustments
- Made the oomoo mold
- Made adjustments
- Test Cast
- Printed Test Interior Pieces
- Tested Casting with Silver Interior Pieces
- Printed Final Interior Pieces
- Final Casts with Copper Pieces

Rings

Design, CAD, & Printing

I started by getting my finger sizes set and started test printing.

Using grasshopper and my finger measurements, I created 3 interior ring sizes and iterated on my design from there.

After a number of test prints, I decided on the more simple cylindar design. It fit best, was easiest to cast, and the concrete turned out to be extremely delicate, so I focused on the sturdiest feeling-- the one that broke least when taking out of the mold.

Molds & Casting

It took a while to get the mold to be just right. I found making the ring part slightly larger and deeper made it much easier to cast without breaking-- but I needed to make sure I didn't compromising on the interior ring size so it would fit corrrectly.

Misprints, Detours, & Adjustments

No major misprints or adjustments here, except that it did take a number of test prints to get just the right ring sizes and interior pieces.

The copper filament did take a while to get used to. A few things that helped were increasing the temperature on the nozzle & bed. It is also extremely easy to break (which I found out the hard way a few times trying to feed it into the printer.

There were quite a few different interior pieces I printed to test to see what worked best. The half and half designs came out the best I think.

Finishing & Finals

I found that the silver PLA filament worked really nicely. It was a little tougher to get the concrete to stick to the copper.

I used polishing paper to file down each final piece as well as each 3D printed mold.

Ring Holders

Design, CAD, & Printing

This was a fairly straightforward design, a round cone with a tip that was 3D printed. However, this proved to be the trickiest mold.

First, I did a bunch of test prints of the caps. I noticed the tip was looking a little unfinished. I slowed down the speed alot thanks to professor's advice, and this helped to make the tips look more "tippy" and less "drippy".

The molds went through a number of iterations. I really had faith in my original design. The smaller cone mold came out great!
