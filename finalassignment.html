Final Project: Concrete & Metal Jewelry Collection


Concept

For my final project, I made a mini jewelry collection. Inspired by the mix of concrete and metal jewelry trend, and seeing that proto pasta sold metal filament, I thought it would be interesting to see if I could mix the 3d printing with casting.

Inspiration

The concrete jewlery inspiration below all are either painted or include metals. My goal was to include 3D printed metals.

Inspiration

Sources: Necklaces, Ring, Ring Holder

After a few concept sketches, I decided to edit down my pieces to:
1 rectangular pendant with 3d printed piece in it
2 ring holders (1 with 3d printed piece in it)
3 rings (2 with 3d print piece in it)

CAD & Printing

Using grasshopper and my finger measurements, I created 3 interior ring sizes and iterated on my design from there.

After a number of test prints, I decided on the more simple cylindar design. It fit best, was easiest to cast, and the concrete turned out to be extremely delicate, so I focused on the sturdiest feeling-- the one that broke least when taking out of the mold.

Molds & Casting

It took a while to get the mold to be just right. I found making the ring part slightly larger and deeper made it much easier to cast without breaking-- but I needed to make sure I didn't compromising on the interior ring size so it would fit corrrectly.
